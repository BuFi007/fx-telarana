# Stablecoin Summer: Earn in Dollars, Borrow the World

Stablecoin Summer is the fx-Telarana campaign for turning idle stablecoins into programmable FX credit.

Instead of farming a new protocol token, users deposit stablecoins, borrow against them responsibly, and earn targeted rewards for useful market activity.

The campaign umbrella is **Stablecoin Summer**. The contribution system underneath it is **Forex Telarana Genesis**.

```text
Forex Telarana Genesis: Map the first cross-chain FX credit network.
```

## Campaign Thesis

Stablecoins are no longer just digital dollars. They are becoming the collateral layer for global FX credit.

fx-Telarana is building a cross-chain FX credit hub where users can:

- Deposit USDC or other supported stablecoins.
- Borrow supported foreign-currency stablecoins such as JPYC, MXNB, AUDF, KRW1, ZCHF, or EURC as markets go live.
- Enter from supported spoke chains through Circle CCTP V2 for Circle assets and Hyperlane routes for selected non-Circle assets.
- Earn rewards for behavior that strengthens real markets: time-weighted deposits, healthy borrowing, repayment, cross-chain intent completion, and referrals.

No native protocol token is required for the campaign.

## Campaign Design Lesson

The useful lesson from Hyperliquid is not "run a points program and promise an airdrop."

The useful lesson is:

```text
Make campaign scoring inseparable from the market behavior the protocol needs.
```

For Hyperliquid, that behavior was trading, liquidity, and protocol usage. For fx-Telarana, the behavior is:

- Stablecoins supplied to the hub.
- FX assets borrowed against healthy collateral.
- Repayments completed cleanly.
- Cross-chain routes completed through supported spokes.
- LP liquidity that improves FX execution.
- Keeper, oracle, dashboard, and integrator work that strengthens the network.

Points should measure contribution to protocol solvency, liquidity, and useful FX flow. They should not measure empty clicks.

## Forex Telarana Genesis Points

Genesis points are an off-chain, non-transferable contribution score derived from verifiable protocol activity and accepted builder/community work.

Onchain activity may include:

- Deposits.
- Borrows.
- Repayments.
- Route completions.
- LP positions.
- Liquidations and keeper actions.

Off-chain scoring may include:

- Anti-Sybil review.
- Accepted bug reports.
- Integrator submissions.
- Referral validation.
- Community and Discord roles.
- Manual adjustments for abuse, duplicate identities, or exceptional contributions.

Genesis points are not ERC-20 tokens, are not transferable, do not represent equity, and do not guarantee an airdrop.

Recommended public framing:

```text
Points are not tokens. Points are a non-transferable contribution score derived from onchain protocol usage and off-chain builder/community contributions.
```

## Genesis Seasons

Stablecoin Summer can run publicly while Forex Telarana Genesis tracks deeper contribution across protocol phases.

### Season 0: Route Cartographers

Goal: test every supported route and surface integration failures before mainnet.

Eligible activity may include:

- Deposit USDC on a spoke.
- Route to the Fuji or selected test hub.
- Open a borrow position.
- Swap or settle into a target FX asset.
- Repay.
- Submit accepted bug reports and route reports.

Narrative:

```text
Map the Forex Telarana.
```

### Season 1: Liquidity Weavers

Goal: attract durable liquidity.

Eligible activity may include:

- Supplying USDC.
- Supplying supported FX stablecoins or approved mocks during testnet.
- LPing in approved FX pools.
- Maintaining depth and uptime over time.
- Keeping collateral healthy.

### Season 2: Borrower Guild

Goal: prove real FX borrowing demand.

Eligible activity may include:

- Borrowing supported FX assets.
- Maintaining healthy LTV.
- Repaying cleanly.
- Simulating payroll, invoice, import/export, remittance, or treasury flows.
- Opening multi-currency credit lines without liquidation.

### Season 3: Hub Guardians

Goal: make builders, keepers, and integrators first-class participants.

Eligible activity may include:

- SDK integrations.
- Frontend widgets.
- Treasury dashboards.
- Agent integrations.
- Payment or invoice flows.
- Liquidation keepers.
- Oracle monitors.
- Accounting exports.

### Season 4: Mainnet Founders

Goal: reward real mainnet contribution after caps, markets, and monitoring are ready.

Eligible activity may include:

- Real liquidity.
- Real borrowing.
- Real repayments.
- Partner integrations.
- Governance, allowlist, rewards, or ecosystem eligibility, if applicable.

Season 4 should only start after final risk caps, market status, monitoring, and reward budgets are published.

## Season 1: Stablecoin Earn Week

Stablecoin Earn Week is for suppliers.

Users deposit USDC into the fx-Telarana hub vault or supported market path and help power FX lending liquidity.

Eligible activity may include:

- Supplying USDC for a minimum campaign duration.
- Maintaining a deposit for 7, 14, or 30 days.
- Entering from a supported spoke chain and completing the hub-side action.
- Referring a real depositor who maintains an eligible position.

Example public offer:

```text
Deposit USDC.
Earn base lending yield plus campaign rewards.
Power global FX credit.
```

## Season 2: FX Credit Week

FX Credit Week is for borrowers.

Users deposit USDC collateral and borrow a supported foreign-currency stablecoin when the relevant market is live.

Eligible activity may include:

- Borrowing JPYC, MXNB, AUDF, KRW1, ZCHF, or EURC against USDC.
- Keeping the position healthy.
- Staying below the campaign LTV threshold.
- Maintaining the position for a minimum number of days.
- Repaying on time without liquidation.

Example public offer:

```text
Borrow MXNB against USDC and receive a borrow-rate rebate while your position stays healthy.
```

The campaign should not reward maximum leverage. It should reward durable, healthy credit behavior.

Recommended baseline eligibility:

```text
LTV below 55%
Position open for at least 7 days
No liquidation during the reward window
Borrow size above the campaign minimum
Oracle deviation within normal bounds
```

## Season 3: Cross-Chain Summer

Cross-Chain Summer is for users who enter from supported spoke chains.

The goal is not to reward raw bridge volume. The goal is to reward completed intents that result in useful market activity.

Eligible activity may include:

- Sending USDC or EURC from a supported CCTP V2 spoke chain.
- Completing a hub action such as supply, supply collateral, repay, borrow, or swap.
- Maintaining the resulting position for the campaign duration.
- Using a supported Hyperlane route only after the route is deployed, classified, and enabled.

Example public offer:

```text
Bring dollars from any supported chain. Borrow local currency from the hub.
```

## What We Will Not Reward

Stablecoin Summer should avoid incentives that create fake activity.

Not eligible:

- Raw bridge volume without a completed hub action.
- Max-LTV borrowing.
- Instant deposit-withdraw loops.
- Wash deposits.
- Same-wallet referral loops.
- Borrow loops designed only to farm incentives.
- Activity in markets that are paused, capped out, or oracle-risk flagged.

Eligible behavior should be time-weighted, health-checked, and auditable onchain.

## Points Formula Direction

Use scoring that rewards quality and duration, not raw size alone.

Example direction:

```text
Contribution Score =
Liquidity Supplied
+ Borrow Demand
+ Repayment Quality
+ Cross-chain Route Usage
+ LP Depth
+ Integrator Impact
+ Bug and Research Contributions
- Sybil and Wash Penalties
```

Examples:

```text
USDC supply points = amount x days supplied x utilization multiplier
Borrow points = borrowed amount x days open x repayment multiplier
FX route points = completed route x route difficulty multiplier
LP points = depth x uptime x fee-generation multiplier
```

Do not reward borrow size by itself. Reward duration, repayment, utilization, and route diversity.

## Risk Notes

All DeFi activity carries risk.

Key risks include:

- Smart contract risk.
- Oracle risk.
- Liquidation risk.
- Stablecoin issuer and depeg risk.
- Bridge and cross-chain messaging risk.
- Market liquidity risk.
- Rewards program changes, caps, and eligibility exclusions.

Borrow rewards may reduce effective borrowing cost, but they do not stop debt from accruing and do not improve a user's LTV. Users remain responsible for maintaining healthy positions.
